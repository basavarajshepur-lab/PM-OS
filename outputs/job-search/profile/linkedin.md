## Banner image

LinkedIn banner spec: **1584 × 396 pixels** (4:1 ratio), JPG or PNG, max 8MB. Sits behind your photo on the profile.

### Three concepts

**Concept 1: Abstract data flow (recommended)**
Subtle network / node graph in deep blue and slate, with soft light highlights. Suggests data, connectivity, and AI without being literal. Sophisticated and on-brand for Banking × Data × AI.

**Concept 2: London skyline silhouette**
Stylized London skyline at dawn or dusk in monochrome blue. Anchors geographic identity for UK roles.

**Concept 3: Typography / headline banner**
Solid dark background (navy or charcoal) with simple sans-serif type. Positioning line like "Banking × Data × AI" or "Senior Product Leader, 19 years." Bold but minimal.

**Recommendation:** Concept 1. Lowest risk, professional, won't feel stale in 6 months.

### How to make it

**Option A: Canva (easiest)**
Use the LinkedIn banner template (1584 × 396). Search "abstract data" or "tech minimal" templates. Customize colors to navy / deep blue palette. Free tier works.

**Option B: AI image generation (Midjourney, DALL-E, Ideogram)**
Try a prompt like:

```
Abstract minimalist network graph, deep navy blue background with subtle light blue highlights, sparse interconnected nodes and lines, soft gradient, professional banking aesthetic, no text, clean composition with negative space on the right side --ar 4:1
```

The "negative space on the right" leaves room for your photo and headline overlay.

**Option C: Free stock + crop**
Unsplash search: "data visualization", "abstract tech", "blue gradient". Pick a 4:1 horizontal image, crop to 1584 × 396.

### Key principles
- No text on the banner (LinkedIn overlays your name and headline already)
- Negative space on the right side (where the photo + headline sit)
- Color palette that doesn't fight your photo
- Subtle, not loud. Professional, not playful.

## Profile photo

LinkedIn photo spec: **400 × 400 pixels minimum** (recommended 800 × 800), square crop, JPG or PNG, max 8MB.

### The 7-point checklist
- [ ] Recent (within 3 years)
- [ ] Face takes 60%+ of the frame
- [ ] Smiling or confidently pleasant expression
- [ ] Plain, non-distracting background (solid wall, blur, or simple natural)
- [ ] Direct eye contact with camera
- [ ] Good lighting (natural or soft studio; avoid harsh shadows)
- [ ] Business casual or business formal (banking norm)

If your current photo passes all 7, keep it. If it fails any, update.

### Three options to update

**Option A: Professional headshot (recommended)**
£150-300 in London for a 1-hour session and ~30 edited shots. Search "LinkedIn headshot photographer London" or check Peerspace and Snappr. Lasting 3+ years; best return on a small investment.

Wear what you'd wear to an exec meeting. Bring two outfit options.

**Option B: AI headshot generator**
£20-50 for 50+ AI-generated professional headshots. Services: Aragon, Headshot Pro, BetterPics. Upload 10-20 selfies, get back a portfolio.

Caveat: 2026 quality is good but some recruiters can spot AI tells. Pick the most natural-looking output and avoid uncanny-valley shots.

**Option C: DIY with iPhone**
Stand by a window in soft natural light (avoid harsh midday sun). Plain wall behind you. Portrait mode, 30+ shots, pick the best. Edit in Lightroom Mobile: slight exposure boost, subtle clarity, neutral tone. Free if you have a partner or a tripod.

**Recommendation:** Option A. You're job-searching at senior level. The photo gets seen by hundreds of recruiters and hiring managers over the next 3-6 months. £200 for a photo that lasts 3 years is the smallest high-impact investment you can make right now.

## Open to Work toggle

LinkedIn has two visibility modes for the Open to Work signal:

- **Recruiters only (recommended for your situation)**: subtle, only paid LinkedIn Recruiter accounts see it. Doesn't tip off Deutsche Bank or your current network.
- **Public (#OPENTOWORK photo frame)**: visible to everyone. Better for unemployed candidates; risky while you're still employed.

### How to enable

1. Go to your profile → click the "Open to" button under your name (or photo)
2. Choose "Finding a new job"
3. Fill in:
   - **Job titles**: Senior Product Manager, Lead Product Manager, Group Product Manager, Director of Product Management
   - **Locations**: London (United Kingdom); United Kingdom; Remote
   - **Job types**: Full-time
   - **Start date**: Immediately (or your preferred date)
   - **Who sees this**: Recruiters only (NOT "All LinkedIn members")
4. Save

The signal gets picked up by recruiters using LinkedIn Recruiter when they search for matching profiles. This is what drives most senior-level inbound.

## Custom URL

LinkedIn defaults to `linkedin.com/in/[name]-[random-numbers]`. Customize it for cleanliness, memorability, and shareability.

### Recommended (in priority order)

1. `linkedin.com/in/basavarajshepur` (cleanest, no separators)
2. `linkedin.com/in/basavaraj-shepur` (with hyphen, also fine)
3. `linkedin.com/in/bshepur` (short fallback if the first two are taken)

Avoid dots, underscores, or special characters. Autocomplete and email systems sometimes mangle them.

### How to set

1. Go to your profile
2. Top right: click "Edit public profile & URL"
3. Right sidebar: find "Edit your custom URL"
4. Click the pencil icon
5. Type your desired URL, then save

Once set, update your custom URL on your resume, email signature, and any business cards.
